About Me

Hi, I’m a 23 years old human-being and I’ve always been passionate about sports 🏃, nature 🌍, art 📽 and tech 💻.
Professionally speaking, I would describe myself as a machine learning, computer vision and robotics enthusiast, who secretly dreams of making the world a better place through technology.

Bio

I recently graduated from a Master of Engineering in Robotics, delivered by Polytech Sorbonne, in Paris, France.
2 years ago, during this formation, I started taking a real interest in artificial intelligence and more specifically in machine learning.
This growing curiosity led me to the ISTeC lab of the University of Nottingham, where I did an internship about accelerometer-based human gesture recognition using machine learning models. My scientific article was accepted and presented during the 2019 IEEE Symposium Series on Computational Intelligence, in Xiamen, China.
After this positive experience, I spent a semester at the University of Montreal, taking courses on machine learning and natural language processing, in order to have theoretical knowledge and a stronger practical experience. I completed this formation by following a MOOC about deep learning, published by the MIT.
I then went back to Paris to validate my Master in Robotics by completing a 6-month internship at FieldBox.ai, where I explored computer vision algorithms to track industrial machines activity and operationalise these solutions through a web application.

Publications
